Main service

Walnut blasting of intake valves and cylinder ports

This is the core of what we do. On modern direct-injection petrol engines, fuel is sprayed straight into the combustion chamber ‚Äì not over the intake valves ‚Äì so deposits build up and choke the air path. We remove that carbon mechanically.

How the walnut blasting process works

We use crushed walnut shell media at carefully controlled pressure to strip carbon from the intake valves and ports without damaging metal surfaces. It‚Äôs a direct, mechanical clean of the exact area that causes hesitation, misfires and power loss.

  • üß∞ Intake manifold removed properly
    We strip the intake manifold and related parts to access each port directly ‚Äì no ‚Äúspray it through the throttle body‚Äù shortcuts.
  • üîí Valves closed & protected
    The engine is turned by hand so only the cylinder being cleaned has closed valves, keeping walnut media out of the combustion chamber.
  • üßπ Controlled blast & extraction
    Specialist wands and extraction remove loosened carbon immediately, keeping ports, cylinders and the engine bay clean.
  • üì∏ Before & after proof
    We borescope or photograph each port so you can see the exact difference on your valves ‚Äì no generic stock photos.

+
Yes ‚Äì when done properly. We close the valves on the cylinder we‚Äôre working on, control the air pressure and use extraction to remove media and carbon as we go. Walnut shell is softer than metal, so it cleans deposits without damaging valve seats or ports when used correctly.

+
