How proper walnut blasting works

We use finely crushed walnut shell media, fired at controlled pressure, to gently strip carbon from intake valves and ports. Walnut shell is abrasive enough to remove stubborn deposits, but soft enough not to damage metal surfaces when used correctly.

  • ‚úî Manifold removed the right way
    We remove the intake manifold and related components for full access to each port ‚Äì no shortcuts through the throttle body.
  • ‚úî Valves locked shut per cylinder

  • ‚úî Controlled blast & extraction
    Our blasting rig and extraction system keep carbon and media contained, leaving ports clean and your engine bay tidy.

Why walnut blasting

Why modern engines suffer from carbon build-up

On direct-injection petrol engines the fuel is sprayed straight into the combustion chamber, not over the back of the valves like older port-injection engines. That means there‚Äôs nothing to wash away oily vapour and exhaust gas recirculation deposits ‚Äì so they stick to the valves and ports and slowly choke the engine.

  • ‚ö° Hesitation and flat spots
    Restricted airflow and uneven mixture cause a ‚Äúdead‚Äù feeling when you try to accelerate, especially from low RPM.
  • üõë Rough idle and cold-start misfires
    Carbon build-up disturbs airflow around the valves, making idle lumpy and triggering occasional misfire codes.
  • ‚õΩ Increased fuel consumption
    The ECU compensates for poor breathing and uneven mixture, and MPG often drops without any obvious fault light.
  • üí® Performance doesn‚Äôt match the spec sheet

Compared to other methods

There are lots of products that claim to ‚Äúde-carbon‚Äù engines ‚Äì HHO machines, intake sprays, fuel additives and more. On modern direct-injection engines, none of these can physically scrub thick carbon off the back of the valves. Walnut blasting can.

  • üö´ HHO / hydrogen carbon cleaning
    These machines feed gas through the intake while the engine runs. They may clean light combustion-chamber deposits but won‚Äôt touch hardened carbon on closed intake valves.
  • üö´ Sprays and ‚Äúintake cleaner‚Äù through the throttle
    With no physical access to the ports, most of the product burns off in the cylinders. At best they soften very light deposits; at worst they loosen chunks without removing them properly.
  • üö´ Fuel additives
    Because fuel in direct-injection engines doesn‚Äôt wash over the valves, tank additives mainly affect injectors and combustion, not the intake valves themselves.
  • ‚úÖ Walnut blasting
    Physically removes deposits from the valve faces, stems and port walls while the valves are safely closed. When done properly it‚Äôs the safest and most effective method available today.
