What we do

Walnut blasting done properly ‚Äì not a ‚Äúquick clean‚Äù gimmick

On modern direct-injection engines, fuel is injected straight into the combustion chamber, not over the back of the valves. Oil vapour and EGR soot stick to the valve stems and ports, slowly choking airflow and upsetting mixture. Walnut blasting is the safest way to remove that carbon when performed correctly.

How we carry out walnut blasting

  • ‚úî Full access, no shortcuts
    Intake manifold removed so each port can be cleaned directly ‚Äì we don‚Äôt just spray through the throttle body and hope for the best.
  • ‚úî Valves locked closed
    The engine is rotated by hand so only the cylinder being blasted has its valves closed, keeping walnut media out of the combustion chamber.
  • ‚úî Controlled media & extraction
    Crushed walnut shell is fired at controlled pressure, with continuous vacuum extraction to lift loosened carbon out of the port.

Why walnut blasting

Why this method beats sprays, additives and HHO ‚Äúcarbon cleans‚Äù

The internet is full of miracle carbon cleaners ‚Äì fuel additives, chemical sprays, even HHO machines claiming to ‚Äústeam clean‚Äù your engine. They may shift light combustion deposits, but they cannot physically remove the heavy, baked-on carbon you see on intake valves and in EGR/intake tracts.

Walnut blasting is both mechanical and targeted. Crushed walnut shells gently hit the carbon from close range and break it away from the metal, while the valves are safely closed. Done correctly, it‚Äôs one of the safest and most effective ways to remove heavy intake deposits without damaging components.
Comparison

Walnut blasting vs other ‚Äúcarbon cleaning‚Äù methods

Here‚Äôs how walnut blasting compares to common alternatives currently on the market.

Walnut blasting

Physically removes heavy carbon from valves, ports and some intake/EGR areas. Safe for metal surfaces when used at the correct pressure with valves closed and extraction in place.

‚úî Safest & most effective for heavy build-up
HHO / hydrogen carbon cleaning

Gasses are fed through the intake while the engine runs. Can help with light combustion deposits but has very limited effect on thick carbon stuck upstream of the combustion chamber ‚Äì especially on intake valves of direct-injection engines.

‚úò Limited effect where the real problem is
Sprays & fuel additives

Additives in the tank or aerosol sprays into the intake may tidy light residues, but they can‚Äôt reach or scrub baked deposits on the back of the valves. In some cases they risk dislodging chunks of carbon with nowhere safe to go.

‚úò Not a solution for serious build-up
